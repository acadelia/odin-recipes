Mushroom Soup

Soups made with cream and mushrooms are much older than the canned variety. Ancient Italian (Salsa colla) and French (Béchamel) cream sauces, and soups based on them have been made for many hundreds of years. In America, the Campbell Soup Company began producing its well-known "Cream of Mushroom Soup" in 1934.

  • 90g butter
  • 2 medium onions, roughly chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 500g mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 1L hot chicken stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 tbsp single cream
  • small handful flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped, to serve (optional)
  1. Start - Heat the butter in a large saucepan and cook the onions and garlic until soft but not browned, about 8-10 mins.
  2. Mushrooms - Add the mushrooms and cook over a high heat for another 3 mins until softened. Sprinkle over the flour and stir to combine.
  3. Chickens - Pour in the chicken stock, bring the mixture to the boil, then add the bay leaf and simmer for another 10 mins.
  4. Blend - Remove and discard the bay leaf, then remove the mushroom mixture from the heat and blitz using a hand blender until smooth.
  5. Last move- Gently reheat the soup and stir through the cream (or, you could freeze the soup at this stage – simply stir through the cream when heating). Scatter over the parsley, if you like, and serve.
